# Globis Edge 2.0 — 3-Minute Video Prompt
## 30-Scene Sequence | Mid-Century Editorial Illustration Style

---

## PART 0: Art Direction & Visual Language

### Reference Style
**Grainy mid-century editorial poster** — handpainted desert archaeology aesthetic. NOT photorealistic. NOT cartoon. Subtly textured, grain-forward, with visible stippling in sand, fabric, and sky.

**Example**: The attached reference image (caseworker with refugee family in desert intake scene, warm ochre and rust palette, teal accents).

### Color Palette (LOCKED)
- **Sand/Base**: Warm yellow-ochre (#E8D7A0), creamy parchment (#F5F1E8)
- **Earth & Rock**: Rust-red (#A84C3C), terracotta (#C67C4A), deep brown (#5C4033)
- **Sky**: Dusty turquoise (#7FAFCA), soft cyan (#A8C9DB), pale cream-white (#FFFAF0)
- **Accent**: Teal (#4A8BA0) — used for Hawa's headscarf, the Pi 5 indicator light, notebook spine, and small UI details
- **Linework**: Deep charcoal (#2C2C2C), soft black (#1A1A1A) for outlines and shadows

### Rendering Technique
1. **Flat, layered color blocks** with soft internal gradients (especially sky and sand)
2. **Visible grain**: Apply 2–4% stipple/grain texture across entire image (like old printing or pastel paper)
3. **Soft edges**: Minimal hard lines; use feathering and grainy transitions between shapes
4. **Ink-like outlines**: Delicate, hand-drawn borders around figures and key objects
5. **No drop shadows, no blur effects** — everything is in one coherent plane

### Characters (FIXED across all 30 scenes)

**Hawa Ahmed** (caseworker, ~35)
- Dark hair, mostly covered by a **teal headscarf** (consistent height, fold, and drape)
- Face: calm, observant, slightly weathered; warm brown skin tone with natural warm highlights
- Body: capable posture, deliberate hand movements, often leaning slightly over the desk
- Clothing: warm sand/khaki long-sleeve shirt, olive or tan outer garment, modest proportions
- Gesture language: hands near documents, pen poised, sometimes hand to chin in thought
- **Always recognizable by teal headscarf alone**, even in silhouette

**Refugee Mother** (undocumented, ~28–35)
- Dignified, calm bearing; wrapped in a neutral robe or long wrap (soft sand, muted rust-brown)
- Face partially veiled or shaded, but eyes should convey trust and slight concern
- Body language: respectful distance from desk, sometimes hand on child's shoulder
- Clothing: layered wraps in warm neutrals; no bright colors, no stereotypes

**Child 1** (~6 years old, undocumented)
- Small stature, standing or sitting near mother
- Clothing: soft earth-tone tunic or wrap, slightly worn but clean
- Posture: curious but cautious, occasionally glancing at Hawa or the desk

**Child 2** (~10 years old, sibling)
- Slightly taller than Child 1, often standing just behind or beside mother
- Clothing: similar warm-toned wrap or loose shirt
- Posture: observant, alert, sometimes watching the device or Hawa's hands

### Props & Objects (LOCKED across scenes)

**The Desk**
- Simple wooden frame, sand-colored, heavily worn but sturdy
- Surface area: ~4 ft × 2 ft, filled with papers, folders, ID cards
- **Teal folder** (spine visible, strong color accent): always present, usually center-right
- Scattered **intake forms** in cream/parchment tones
- **ID cards** (tan cardboard, small photos, borders in rust or black ink)
- **Pen and pencil** in a simple cup or beside the papers
- Under the desk: small wooden crate or shelf (implied, not always visible)

**The Pi 5 Device**
- **Physical form**: compact box (~15 cm × 10 cm × 6 cm), muted sand-tone casing
- **Visible ports**: hint of a camera module on top (small rectangular indent), microphone port implied
- **Teal indicator light**: single small circle (LED-sized) on the front, glows softly (most important visual)
- **No screen of its own**; the device has a small angled display tray (abstract screen shape, no text, just teal and sand rectangular panels)
- **Placement**: usually on desk surface, near the teal folder, often with papers partially overlapping its edges
- **Visual weight**: small and humble; never dominates the frame. Should feel like a tool, not a protagonist

**Optional Screen/Display**
- Simple rectangular angled display on a small stand, or integrated into the desk tray
- **No text, no logos, no legible UI**: only abstract teal and sand-colored rectangular shapes and panels
- Sits at ~45° angle toward Hawa so she can glance at it
- Size: modest, ~tablet-sized, never fills more than 20% of the frame

**Background Elements** (recur across all scenes)
- **Canvas tents**: 2–3 simple khaki/cream fabric structures in the mid-ground, edges frayed and stippled for texture
- **Fencing**: low horizontal fencing (simple wood or branches) defining the intake area
- **Sandy foreground**: heavily stippled, with small shadows and texture variation
- **Rocky ridges**: warm rust-reds and terracottas in the far background, simplified silhouettes
- **Sky**: vast, calm, either pale turquoise or dusk tones; add a pale sun or moon (quarter or full, cream-colored)
- **Distant figures**: 2–4 tiny human silhouettes waiting or walking, reinforcing the scale and continuity of the work
- **Dust/atmospheric grain**: overall light haze suggesting heat and aridity

### Continuity Rules
1. **Every scene picks up from the final frame of the previous one**: smooth camera angle shift or lateral pan, never a hard cut or teleport.
2. **Hawa's headscarf, face proportion, and clothing remain identical** across all 30 scenes (even if partially visible).
3. **Desk, papers, teal folder, and Pi 5 device appear in ~28 of the 30 scenes** (scenes 27–28 may zoom very close or zoom very far, so elements may be cropped or distant).
4. **No new props or unrelated visual motifs** introduced. No text overlays, no logos, no labels, no subtitles.
5. **Paper motion**: wind-blown edges, slight rustling, papers sliding under hands—subtle but consistent with the desert breeze.
6. **Light direction**: warm sun, long shadows in the foreground and on the desk, soft glow on faces; sky darkens gradually in later scenes (dusk progression).
7. **Grain and texture**: apply the same stippled, mid-century aesthetic to every scene. No smooth rendering.

### Camera & Motion Rules
1. **Speed**: All motion is slow and deliberate. No quick cuts, no whip pans. 5-second scenes allow for creeping camera moves and subtle gesture changes.
2. **Move Types**:
   - Gentle push-in (toward desk or face)
   - Slow pull-back (widening the view)
   - Lateral pans (sliding left or right across the desk or landscape)
   - Subtle tilts (angle up slightly toward sky or down toward papers)
   - Overhead transitions (camera angle shifts from side view to top-down or vice versa)
3. **Motion in the world** (not the camera):
   - Paper edges lift and settle in the breeze
   - Hawa's hands move deliberately across papers or near the device
   - Child shifts weight or steps closer
   - Mother's gaze or posture shifts slightly
   - Dust motes or fabric folds flutter
4. **Framing priorities**:
   - Lower third: desk, papers, device, hands
   - Middle third: Hawa's torso and face (varies by scene)
   - Upper third: horizon, tents, sky
5. **Depth**: Use layering—close desk objects in sharp detail, mid-ground tents slightly softer, far background very simplified.

## PART 1: Scenes 1–10 — Problem & Context (Cold Open)

Pale sun in a turquoise, speckled sky. Wind ripples through the mother's wrap.

**Duration**: 5 seconds
**Camera Move**: Slow, subtle push-in toward Hawa and the desk. Start at a wide angle (~50mm equivalent) and gradually tighten to medium-wide (~35mm) by the end of the 5 seconds. A light desert breeze moves a few paper corners and fabric folds. The mother shifts her weight slightly and one child steps a little closer, drawn by the desk and the caseworker.

**Mood**: Calm observation. No urgency, no chaos. This is routine, but it matters.

## PART 5: Post-Production Assembly

**Duration**: 3 minutes = 180 seconds
**Frame Rate**: 24 fps (cinema standard, or 30 fps for smoother motion if needed)
**Resolution**: 1920 × 1080 (Full HD) or 3840 × 2160 (4K if possible)

**Audio Stack**:
1. **Ambient**: Gentle desert wind (subtle, always present, ~-20 dB)
2. **SFX**: Paper rustling, pen scratches, subtle device beep/pulse (~-15 dB)
3. **Music**: Minimal, warm orchestral or ambient (strings, sustained notes, ~-10 dB during the visual-only sections)
4. **Voice-over** (optional, ~-5 dB): 
   - Short intro or outro in English + one Chadian language (Arabic, French) emphasizing "frontier dignity" or "local power"
   - Keep VO sparse; let the visuals tell the story
5. **Silence**: Use 1–2 seconds of pure silence in Scenes 12, 18 to emphasize the weight of decisions
