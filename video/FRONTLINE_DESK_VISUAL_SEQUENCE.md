# Frontline Desk Visual Sequence

**Format:** 30 clips x 5 seconds = 150 seconds total  
**Use case:** storyboard, image-to-video prompt pack, or shot list for assembly into a continuous film  
**Constraint:** no visible text, no logos, no subtitles

## Global Style Bible

Use this exact style in every clip:

- Landscape illustration in the style of a grainy mid-century editorial poster, similar to a hand-painted desert archaeology scene
- Bold simplified shapes, strong silhouettes, layered flat color blocks, heavy stippled texture and pointillist grain across sand, clothing, desk, and sky
- Warm sand-yellow and ochre base, rich oranges and rust reds in the landscape, muted turquoise and dusty cyan sky, deep teal accents on clothing and objects, darker browns and charcoals for linework and shadow
- Flat but layered rendering with visible paper-like grain, soft sky gradients, delicate ink-like outlines, minimal hard edges, no photorealism
- Calm, human, observational mood with quiet dignity and zero sensationalism

Keep these visual constants in every clip:

- Hawa: frontline caseworker, mid-30s, calm face, dark hair mostly covered by a modest teal headscarf, simple field clothing in sand, khaki, and olive tones
- Refugee mother: dignified adult woman in a neutral robe or wrap
- Two children: close to the mother, one slightly nearer the desk, one slightly behind
- Desk: simple wooden desk in the lower third whenever possible, with scattered papers, intake forms, ID cards, and a teal folder or notebook
- Device: a small grounded local device on the desk, low-profile box shape, muted sand-colored casing, single teal indicator light
- Optional small screen angled toward Hawa, with abstract shapes and panels only, never legible text
- Background: sparse desert reception setting with sand, a few canvas tents, low fencing, distant silhouettes, warm rocky ridges, pale sun or moon in a turquoise sky
- Parchment-like texture, stippled grain, and the same warm palette across all scenes

## Continuity Rules

- Keep Hawa's face, proportions, teal headscarf, and clothing consistent across all 30 clips
- Keep the mother and both children consistent in size, clothing language, and spatial relationship to the desk and horizon
- Keep the desk, papers, teal folder, device, and distant tents or ridges present in most clips, even when partly cropped
- Each clip must begin from the final frame of the previous clip and only adjust camera angle, framing, or small gestures
- Camera movement must stay slow and simple: gentle push-in, pull-back, lateral pan, slight tilt, low-angle slide, or overhead transition
- World motion must remain subtle: breeze on papers, fabric movement, posture shifts, and slow light changes
- Do not add new props, extra signage, UI text, futuristic motifs, or unrelated symbols

## Negative Prompt

Avoid photorealism, glossy sci-fi hardware, neon lighting, sharp modern UI, visible text, subtitles, logos, dramatic action, war imagery, weapons, crowds in panic, excessive clutter, extra props, extra children, costume changes, camera shake, jump cuts, comic-book exaggeration, or inconsistent faces.

## Assembly Notes

- Generate all clips in landscape format with the same aspect ratio and the same seed or reference-character setup when possible
- Treat the final frame of each clip as the image reference for the next clip
- Keep Hawa as the visual anchor of the sequence; if any shot drifts too far from her likeness, regenerate before proceeding
- Preserve the teal folder and teal indicator light as the recurring continuity markers
- If a generator struggles with continuity, first create still keyframes for all 30 scenes, lock character references, then animate each still with subtle image-to-video motion
